Diploma in Horticulture

Diploma in Horticulture may have an average duration of two years. The course is equivalent to the graduate degree in agriculture dealing with the study of crops, plantation, flower farming, etc.
Diploma in Horticulture is a specific field of agriculture that involves plantation and the management of the plants. However, agriculture is a broad area where the candidates study crops and animals and their management. Diploma in Horticulture teaches about plant cultivation, flowers, trees, gardening, and different insects and worms which affect the plants.

Diploma in Horticulture: What is it about?

The duration of a diploma in Horticulture is from one year to two years. Horticulture Diploma is all about plant cultivation and the management of plants. Horticulture is a specific branch of plantation in which the students study the methodologies and techniques involved in the cultivation of plants, flowers, and crops. The Horticulture field can be divided into three categories: Pomology, ornamental horticulture, and Olericulture. The candidates pursuing the diploma in Horticulture make their career in the fields of home gardening, arboriculture, urban gardening, and floriculture.

Diploma in Horticulture: Admission Process

The Colleges/Universities have different criteria for admission into Diploma in Horticulture course.

  • Some Colleges/Universities provide the admission to Diploma in Horticulture course the performance in the qualifying exam.
  • Some Colleges/Universities offer direct admissions based on the overall academic performance.
  • Some Colleges/Universities offer admission based on the performance in the Entrance Exam.

Diploma in Horticulture: Eligibility Criteria

The candidates must go through the eligibility criteria of the college before they apply for Diploma in Horticulture course.

  • The candidates who are seeking admissions to Diploma in Horticulture course must have completed class 10 from a Govt. recognized college.
  • The candidates are required to achieve at least 35% marks in the qualifying exam.
  • Some colleges offer a special reservation for the candidates who are from a farmer family.
  • The candidates must be between the age of 15 years to 22 years.
  • The candidates below the minimum age can also apply for the horticulture diploma course after producing an age relaxation certificate.

Diploma in Horticulture: Entrance Exam

There is no specific Entrance exam to get admission into the Diploma in Horticulture course. The Colleges/Universities conduct their Entrance exams to offer admission into the diploma course. The HORTICET exam is conducted to offer admissions into the B.Sc. Horticulture course and the candidates having the Diploma in Horticulture get 15% seats in the course.
